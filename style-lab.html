Ten homepage directions, including quieter dark studies and font experiments.

Each version uses the same core content but changes hierarchy, tone, pacing, and visual language. The latest set focuses on restrained type scale and different font personalities instead of louder layouts.

01 Editorial

Warm, polished, and expressive.

The current direction. Strong hero typography, softer atmosphere, and a premium-feeling grid.

Open style

 02 Atlas

Structured and map-like.

More compositional and spatial. Good if you want a thoughtful, systems-oriented feel without getting too corporate.

Open style

 03 Signals

Sharper and more technical.

A bolder, more compressed layout with clearer operator energy and a stronger engineering tone.

Open style

 04 Notebook

Lighter, calmer, and more personal.

A simpler essay-like homepage that feels more intimate and less like a startup landing page.

Open style

 05 Nocturne

Dark, serif-led, and continuous.

More like a personal statement than a homepage. Minimal structure, grayscale mood, and almost no visible categorization.

Open style

 06 Dossier

Sparse, document-like, and restrained.

Feels closer to a memo or profile sheet. Minimal decoration, dark theme, and a more serious tone.

Open style

 07 Monolith

Bold, grayscale, and statement-first.

Higher contrast and more severe. Good if you want the site to feel unmistakably minimal but still memorable.

 08 Quiet Sans

Minimal, dark, and understated.

A softer sans-serif direction with calmer hierarchy and less visual pressure.

 09 Quiet Serif

Editorial, but subdued.

Uses serif type for warmth without the dramatic scale of the earlier concepts.

 10 Quiet Mono
